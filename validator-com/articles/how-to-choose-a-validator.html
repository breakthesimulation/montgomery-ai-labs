How to Choose a Validator — The Staker's Framework

You're about to stake your hard-earned SOL. But there are 1,800+ validators. Picking wrong could cost you money — or worse, expose you to centralized risk. Here's how to separate the signal from the noise.

The Five Criteria Framework

Not all validators are equal. Use these five criteria to evaluate any validator.

1. Uptime

Uptime measures how often the validator is working. Look for 99.9%+ uptime over the past few epochs. A validator at 99% sounds good, but that means 3.5 days of downtime per year. That's real money lost.

Check both recent uptime and historical performance. Some validators had problems in the past. If they've recovered, that's okay. But if they've been flaky recently, avoid them.

2. Commission

Validator commission is their cut of the staking rewards. A validator charging 10% gives you 90% of the rewards. One charging 5% gives you 95%.

The sweet spot is 7-10%. Rates below 5% may be unsustainable. Rates above 12% are expensive. But a high-commission validator with excellent infrastructure might be worth it.

3. Stake Concentration

This is crucial for decentralization. If one validator holds too much SOL, they have too much power. Look at how much total SOL is staked with them.

Validators with over 5% of total stake are concerning. The top 10 validators combined shouldn't control more than 25-30% of stake. When you stake, you're supporting decentralization.

4. Infrastructure

How does the validator run their node? Quality infrastructure means better uptime and security.

Look for information about their data center, hardware, and redundancy. Professional validators publish this info. If they don't share anything, that's a red flag.

Good validators have backups, monitoring, and fast network connections. They respond quickly when problems occur.

5. Community

Active Discord servers, transparent communication, and helpful responses all matter. Good validators engage with their stakers.

Check if they respond to questions. See if they post updates about their operations. Community trust takes time to build.

Understanding Validator Metrics

Now that you know what to look for, let's decode the actual numbers.

APY Calculation

Staking APY on Solana runs around 6-8%. Your actual return depends on the validator's commission. If the network pays 7% and validator charges 8% commission, you get:

7% × (1 - 0.08) = 6.44%

Some wallets show "effective APY" after commission. This helps compare validators directly.

What "Delinquent" Means

If a validator misses too many votes, they become "delinquent." This means they're not doing their job. A delinquent validator won't earn rewards, and neither will you.

Always check the delinquent status. It's usually red and obvious in any validator explorer.

Why Stake Concentration Matters

Decentralization isn't just a buzzword. If a few validators control most of the stake, they could potentially censor transactions or influence the network.

When you stake with an already-dominant validator, you're making concentration worse. Choose smaller validators to help balance the network.

Red Flags to Avoid

Watch out for these warning signs.

0% Commission

Some validators charge 0%. This sounds great. But how do they pay for infrastructure? Either they're losing money (unsustainable) or there's a catch.

Be skeptical of 0%. A small commission (5-8%) is more realistic for a sustainable operation.

Single Data Center

If a validator doesn't disclose their infrastructure, that's a problem. Even worse: if all their nodes are in one building. Hardware fails. Networks go down. Redundancy matters.

No Public Information

Validators who don't share their setup are harder to trust. Professional operations publish details about their data centers and hardware.

History of Downtime

Check for past incidents. A validator might have had a rough start but recovered. Or they might have ongoing problems. Look at the last 30-90 days of performance.

Slash History

If a validator has been slashed before, find out why. Slashing means they broke protocol rules and lost staked SOL. A single incident might be explainable. Multiple incidents suggest incompetence.

Building a Diversified Validator Set

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Here's why diversification matters.

Validator Failure

Things go wrong. Validators go offline. Sometimes they close entirely. If all your SOL is with one validator, you're stuck waiting for unbonding.

By spreading across 3-5 validators, you reduce your exposure to any single point of failure.

Decentralization Support

Your staking choices affect the network. Staking with smaller validators helps decentralization. Staking with the biggest validators makes them bigger.

This isn't just philosophical. A decentralized network is more secure and resilient.

Recommended Approach

Stake with 3-5 validators. Include some with lower commission (5-7%), some with average (7-10%). Mix larger established validators with smaller ones.

Rebalance occasionally. If a validator's performance drops, move your stake. This keeps your returns healthy.

Tools for Validator Research

You don't need to do this manually. Several tools make validator research easy.

Solana Beach (solanabeach.io) shows detailed validator stats. You can sort by uptime, commission, stake size, and more. It's the most comprehensive tool.

SolanaFM (solana.fm) offers similar data with a cleaner interface. Good for quick checks.

Staking rewards calculators help estimate your returns. Multiply your stake by the effective APY to see expected earnings.

Many wallets let you compare validators directly. Phantom, Solflare, and Ledger all integrate with the network and show key metrics.

FAQ

What is the best Solana validator?

The best validator depends on your priorities. Look for 99.9%+ uptime, 7-10% commission, and reasonable stake size. The "best" varies by your needs.

How do I check validator uptime?

Use Solana Beach or SolanaFM. Look for 99.9%+ uptime over recent epochs. Check both current and historical performance.

Should I choose low commission validators?

Not necessarily. Very low commission (0-3%) may be unsustainable. 7-10% is the healthy range. Focus on uptime and infrastructure quality.

What is validator commission?

Commission is the validator's cut of staking rewards. If a validator charges 10% commission, they keep 10% and you receive 90%.

How many validators should I stake with?

Stake with 3-5 validators. This provides diversification and supports decentralization.
